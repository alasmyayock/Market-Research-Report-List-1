<p><p>The agricultural plow market comprises several types, each suited for specific soil conditions and farming practices. Reversible plows allow for efficient soil flipping, while disc plows excel in tough, rocky terrain. Ridge plows enable farmers to create raised beds for better drainage, and chisel plows are designed for deep tillage without turning the soil over entirely. Moldboard plows, traditional in design, effectively invert soil layers, enhancing aeration and weed control. Each type addresses unique agricultural needs, influencing market demand and innovation.</p></p>
