<p><p>High-Fructose Corn Syrup (HFCS) is categorized primarily into two types: HFCS 42 and HFCS 55. HFCS 42 contains 42% fructose and is commonly used in processed foods and beverages, such as salad dressings and snacks. HFCS 55, with 55% fructose, is primarily utilized in soft drinks and sweetened beverages due to its sweeter profile. Both types are derived from corn starch, and their applications vary based on sweetness, cost, and product formulation preferences in the food and beverage industry.</p></p>
