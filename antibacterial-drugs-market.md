<p><p>The antibacterial drugs market encompasses various classes, each targeting specific bacterial infections. Cephalosporins are broad-spectrum antibiotics effective against multiple bacteria and are often used for respiratory and skin infections. Penicillins are one of the earliest discovered and are effective against gram-positive bacteria. Fluoroquinolones are potent against a wide range of pathogens, often prescribed for urinary and respiratory infections. Macrolides are used primarily for respiratory tract infections and are known for fewer side effects. Carbapenems are reserved for severe infections due to their effectiveness against resistant bacteria. "Others" may include newer or less common antibiotic classes.</p></p>
