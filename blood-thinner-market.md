<p><p>The blood thinner market is divided into two main types: injectable and oral blood thinners. Injectable blood thinners, often used in hospitals or for immediate anticoagulation, include medications like heparin and low molecular weight heparins. Oral blood thinners, such as warfarin and newer direct oral anticoagulants, are taken at home for long-term management of conditions like atrial fibrillation and venous thromboembolism. Both types are essential for preventing blood clots and managing cardiovascular risks, catering to different patient needs and treatment settings.</p></p>
