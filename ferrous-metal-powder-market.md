<p><p>The ferrous metal powder market is primarily segmented into two types based on compaction methods: cold compaction and hot compaction. Cold compaction involves pressing metal powders at room temperature, resulting in cost-effective production with precise dimensional control and minimal oxidation. In contrast, hot compaction uses elevated temperatures to enhance plasticity, allowing for denser, stronger parts with improved mechanical properties. Both methods cater to different applications, balancing factors like production costs, efficiency, and the desired final product characteristics.</p></p>
