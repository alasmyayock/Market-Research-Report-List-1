<p><p>The tennis ball market comprises three primary types: high pressure, low pressure, and pressureless balls. High pressure balls are designed for tournament play, offering a lively bounce due to their tightly sealed rubber core. Low pressure balls use a thicker felt and are favored by beginners for their slower, more controlled gameplay. Pressureless balls maintain consistent performance over time, making them ideal for practice and recreational use, as they do not lose air like traditional balls. Each type serves distinct player needs.</p></p>
