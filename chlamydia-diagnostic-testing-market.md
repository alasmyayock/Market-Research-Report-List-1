<p><p>The Chlamydia diagnostic testing market primarily features two types: Nucleic Acid Amplification Tests (NAATs) and Direct Fluorescent Tests (DFT). NAATs are highly sensitive and specific, allowing for the detection of Chlamydia trachomatis DNA or RNA, making them the preferred choice in clinical settings. In contrast, Direct Fluorescent Tests involve the use of labeled antibodies to identify the bacteria in patient samples. While DFTs are faster, they are less sensitive than NAATs, influencing their usage in diagnostics.</p></p>
